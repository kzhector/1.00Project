Wildfires and Biodiversity

A Brief Study of the Impact of Wildfires on Biodiversity in the United States

Temporal 1992 1993 1994 1995 1996 1997 1998 1999 2000 2001 2002 2003 2004 2005 2006 2007 2008 2009 2010 2011 2012 2013 2014 2015

Introduction

The purpose of this project was to develop a better understanding of how a wildfire impacts the biodiversity of an area. The data used in this project was from the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF) and the fire data is from a kaggle database. From this data I found that wildfires had no impact on the biodiversity of the area in which they occurred.

Using This Website/Visualizing This Data

Use any of the above navigations to go to the year you would like to look at. Each popup is a different fire that took place in that year. Click on the popups to see the fire date in yyyy-mm-dd format, the number of acres burned, the top 10 species in the area before and after the fire, and the change in the number of species. Unfortunately, the temporal data was not able to be visualized properly using folium and a flask server, so if you would like to utilize the temporal map with an active slider, go to the github repository and look at the notebook.
